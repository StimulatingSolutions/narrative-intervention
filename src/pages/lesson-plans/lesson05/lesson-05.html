Integrated Lesson #5

Objectives: Upon completion of the following lesson students will be able to:

  • Students explain/verbalize what goals, attempts, outcomes are (review/reinforce)

  • Students identify goals, attempts, outcomes for protagonist (review/reinforce)

  • Establish that goals are important to a story

  • Identify simple causes and events as they relate to attempts and outcomes (review/reinforce)

  • Recall/retell text using graphic organizers (build working memory strategies) (secondary)

  • Create and orally tell a detailed story using a graphic organizer (model how to use a graphic organizer to tell/retell stories)

Setting Up for the Magician Narrative

After the students have entered the classroom, set up the props for the Magician Narrative. Have each prop in one of three rooms previously decided on. ROOM 1 will have the 3 paper mâché balloons, ROOM 2 will have the 3-balloon science experiment/lung, and ROOM 3 will have the 2 laminated balloons. The room where the balloons were left will have a note that says “Sorry, I had to move your balloons. I left them…” The rest of the note should be smudged with water/coffee so it is unreadable.

1. Review/Explicit Instruction

  1. Review rules chart and behavior management system.

    Ask students to share any places they have seen goals, tries, or outcomes since our last lesson and ask them why they had those goals/made those tries/got those outcomes.

    Say, “Yesterday, I had the goal of finding a snack to eat. For my first try, I looked in the pantry. Unfortunately, there were no snacks in the pantry. So that try didn’t work. It was an outcome fail. My second try was the refrigerator where I found carrots! I love carrots! This was an outcome yes, because my try worked and I had delicious carrots for my afternoon snack.”

  2. Explain to students that we will now be switching to “I Do/You Help.”

    “We are now going to switch to ‘I Do/You Help.’

    [Place these roles onto the Roles Board.]

    Sometimes when we are working together, once I think that you are getting the hang of it, I will give you a chance to help me out. We will call this time: ‘I Do/You Help’.”

    [Point to the “I Do/You Help” on the Roles Board.]

    “When it is time for ‘I Do/You Help,’ I will still be teaching, but you will be able to participate by offering your ideas or helping me figure out the answers. You should still be listening with your ears,

    [point to ears]

    watching with your eyes,

    [point to eyes]

    and thinking with your brain.

    [point to brain]

    When you have something to add you can let me know by raising your hand.

    [raise hand]

    This will help you think and learn the information by participating more—it will also help me to know what you understand and what you still need help on.”

    [Use Gestures for Explaining Roles in Base Appendix, p. 16.]

  3. Remind students that in the last session we learned to identify outcomes. Explain that today we will continue to learn how to identify goals, tries, and outcomes. We will still be remembering to ask our why questions to understand what caused a character to do something.

  4. Use the script below to remind students about declarative, procedural, and conditional knowledge associated with events, causes, goals, tries and outcomes and the GTO manipulatives, and review the Magician Narrative from Lesson 4.

  5. Review simple causal connections. Say, “Let’s review what we have learned so far about EVENTS, CAUSES, GOALS, TRIES, and OUTCOMES:

    • Ask, “What is an event?”

      [Events are the things that happen in a story.]

    • Ask, “What question do we ask ourselves to help find the events in a story?”

      [What happened?”]

      [Make “what happened” gesture]

    • Ask, “Sometimes some events cause other events to happen. What is a cause?”

      [A cause explains why something happened in a story.]

    • Ask, “What question do we ask ourselves to find the cause?”

      [Why did this happen?]

      [Make “why” gesture]

    • Ask, “How do we show causal connections on our Velcro board?”

      [We show a causal connection using a Because String]

    Review how to find the GOAL….

    • Ask: “What is a goal?”

      [Something a character wants to do or get.]

    • Ask: “When do we find a goal in a story?”

      [The beginning of the story.]

    • Ask: “When we find a goal, what card do we hold up?”

      [Hold up your goal card]

    • Ask: “How do we record the goal on our Velcro board?”

      [Record the goal on one of the yellow thought bubbles.]

      [Display the thought bubble and the goal manipulative]

    • Why do we need to find a goal in a story?

      [Because a goal is the important part of a story. Knowing a goal in a story helps us to remember and understand the story better.]

  6. Review how to find the initiating event using the Because Card. Say,

    • After we figure out the goal, we ask ourselves what?

      [Students respond. Why does the character have this goal?]

      [Make “why” gesture]

      This helps us think about what CAUSED the character to have that goal.

    • “Once we have figured out what caused the character to have their goal, what do we write it on?”.

      [Students respond: A blue Because Card]

    • “After I write the CAUSE on the blue Because card I put it where?

      [Students respond. On the Velcro board next to the goal.]

    • “This will remind us of what CAUSED the character to have the goal. We place this Because Card on the Velcro board,

      [hold up the blue Because Card]

      and we put it right up here next to the goal.”

      [Place the blue Because card to the LEFT of the thought bubble.]

    • “Since this is a CAUSE, we can connect the blue Because card to what it CAUSED. So, we can use a Because String to connect the blue Because card to the goal.”

      [Point to wikki stick connecting the Because card to a thought bubble.]

    • We can retell the story by saying, “I want to...” and tell what the goal is, and then say “because” and know WHY the character wanted to do that..

      [Trace finger along the Because String while saying because]

    Next, review finding TRIES:

    • Ask the students, “Once we know what a character’s goal is, what do we look for next?”
      [What a character did to try to get their goal.]

    • Ask: “What is a try?”

      [Tries are actions/things a character does to try to get what they want/the goal.]

    • Ask, “When we see a try in the story, what card do we hold up?”

      [Hold up your try card]

    • Ask, “How do I record the tries on the Velcro board?”

      [Write the try on a try card.]

    • Ask, “What question do we need to ask ourselves after we find a try?”

      [Why did the character try that?/What caused the character to try that?]

      [Make “why” gesture]

      [Point to the WHY question on the procedure card.]

    • When we think about WHY we TRIED something, it means there is a causal connection. What do we use to connect what the character TRIED and WHY they tried it?

      [A Because string]

    Next, review finding OUTCOMES:

    • Ask the students, “After we find a try, what do we look for next?”
      [What was the outcome of the try.]

    • Ask: “What is an outcome?”

      [An outcome is the result of a try.]

    • Ask: “What is an outcome fail?”

      [The character did not get their goal.]

    • Ask, “When we see an outcome fail, what card do we hold up?”

      [Hold up your outcome fail card.]

    • Ask, “How do I record an outcome fail on the Velcro board?”

      [Write what happened on an outcome fail card.]

    • Ask, “What is an outcome yes?”

      [The character did get their goal.]

    • Ask, “When we see an outcome yes, what card do we hold up?”

      [Hold up your outcome yes card.]

    • Ask, “How do I record an outcome yes on the Velcro board?”

      [Write what happened on an outcome yes card.]

    • Ask, “What question do we need to ask ourselves after we find an outcome?”

      [Why did this outcome happen?]

      [Make “why” gesture]

      [Point to the WHY question on the procedure card.]

    • When we think about WHY we had an OUTCOME, we are looking for a causal connection. What do we use to connect what the character’s OUTCOME was and WHY they had this OUTCOME?

      [A Because string]

    • Ask, “Once we have found a try and outcome, what question do we ask next?”

      [Is the character’s goal still the same?]

  7. Refer students to procedural knowledge cards. “These cards will help us to remember how to find causal connections, and goals, tries, and outcomes in a story. If you need a reminder at any time, you can look at the card and see the steps.” Review the steps of finding goals/tries/outcomes with the students using procedural knowledge card.

  8. Review using the Velcro board. Say, “Remember, the Velcro board can help us to retell a story. We record all the important things in a story, the goals, tries, and outcomes, and then we can look back at them to tell the story again.”

  9. Quickly review the Magician Narrative with students.

2. Magician Narrative: Part II Teacher Modeling/Guided Practice

  1. Explain to students that we will now be switching to “I Do/You Help.”

    “We are now going to switch to ‘I Do/You Help.’

    [Place these roles onto the Roles Board.]

    That means I’ll be doing some of the talking and explaining, and you all will be helping me out. You will be listening with your ears,

    [point to ears]

    watching with your eyes,

    [point to eyes]

    thinking with your brain,

    [point to brain]

    and raising with your hand.

    [raise your hand]

    [Use Gestures for Explaining Roles]

  2. When we left off, we had to leave our balloons in the room because we could not get them to Alex the Magician. Now, the balloons aren’t there. In their place, you find the note telling you that the balloons have been moved.

    “Oh no! Someone moved our balloons. That isn’t a problem, but the part of the note that says where they moved the balloons has been smudged and we can’t read it anymore. They must be still in the school somewhere, so we’ll just have to go look for them.”

    Guide students to determine what our goal is and use goal manipulatives.

    [Students should hold up goal cards. Show the new little goal on a thought bubble and place it on the Velcro board under the other thought bubble.]

    Ask, “What is our goal?”

    [To find all 8 of the balloons]

    “Why do we have this goal?”

    [Make “why” gesture]

    [Because Alex the Magician needs them to practice her trick for the party.] Use the Because string to show causal connections on the Velcro board.

    Explain that now that we have a new goal, we have to make a try at achieving that goal. So, our goal is that we want to find our balloons.

    If students want to know who moved the balloons:

    If students become preoccupied with wanting to know who moved the balloons or why, remind them of our actual goal. The person who moved our balloons left a note, so they obviously weren’t doing it to be mean. Our goal is to find the balloons and take them to Alex the Magician. This is the important part, not finding out who did it.

    “I guess that means we will have to go on a journey to find our balloons somewhere in the school. Remind students that, if we are going to go out of the room we will need to make sure we stay together and do not run or be disruptive in the hallway.” Explain that the same rules that are in effect in our classroom will be used in the hallway. That means that we will walk. We will also keep quiet in case other groups are working.

    Explain that we will take our Velcro board with us so that we can chart out our tries and outcomes as we go.

    [Take Velcro board and manipulatives to use along the way]

    Have students take their try and outcomes cards with them so they can raise them when they see a try or outcome. Have students suggest various places to look.

    Activity Overview:

    You will make three tries: LOCATION 1 (3 balloons have been paper mâchéd), LOCATION 2 (3 balloons have been used in a science experiment/lung), and LOCATION 3 (2 balloons have been laminated) using the following procedure:

    1. Write the try on a blank try card. Ask, “what happened?”

      [Make “what happened” gesture]

    2. Have students place the try card where it should go on the Velcro board.

    3. Have students report the outcome and chart it on the Velcro board.

    Students should take their GTO cards along on search. The co-teacher will follow with Velcro board and cards to record students answers

  3. LOCATION 1

    “Let’s try to find our balloons in.

    [Location 1]

    [Students hold up try card]

    “Oh, I see some try cards. What is our try?”

    [Looking in Location 1]

    Have co-teacher record on card and place on Velcro board.

    Ask, “Why are we trying this?”

    [Make “why” gesture]

    [Because (Make finger move) We want to find all 8 balloons] Use the Because string to show causal connections on Velcro board.

    In Location 1, you find 3 paper mâché balloons. “Oh no! Here are three of our balloons, but they look like they have become someone’s craft project. They are covered in paper mâché!”

    What was the outcome of our first try? What happened?

    [Make “what happened” gesture]

    [We found the balloons, but they had been covered with paper mâché and Alex the Magician won’t be able to use them.]

    Ask, “Why did this outcome happen?”

    [Make “why” gesture]

    [Because we looked in Location 1, which caused (Make finger move) us to see that they had been covered with paper mâché.] Refer to the Because string to make the causal connections on the Velcro board.

    So, did we achieve our goal?. [Well kind of. We found 3 of the 8 balloons, but the balloons were covered in paper mâché and Alex the Magician won’t be able to use them. So, this is an Outcome Fail [Hold up manipulative]

    Is our goal still the same? Yes. We still need to find all 8 balloons and get them to Alex the Magician.

  4. LOCATION 2

    “Let’s see if we can find any more of our balloons in.

    [Location 2]

    [students hold up try manipulative]

    “Oh, I see some try cards. What is our try?”

    [Looking in Location 2]

    Have co-teacher record on card and place on Velcro board.

    Ask, “Why do we need to try this?”

    [Make “why” gesture]

    [Because we did not find all 8 balloons last time/Because our goal is to find all 8 of the balloons] Refer to the Because string to make the causal connections on the Velcro board.

    [Make “what happened” gesture]

    Ask, “Why did this outcome happen?”

    [Make “why” gesture]

    [Because we looked in Location 2, which caused (Make finger move) us to see that the balloons were used in someone's science experiment.] Refer to the Because string to make the causal connections on the Velcro board.

    So, did we achieve our goal this time?. [Well kind of. We found a few more of our balloons, but they were used in a science experiment. Alex the Magician won’t be able to use them to do her trick. This is an Outcome Fail [Hold up Outcome Fail manipulative]

    Is our goal still the same? Yes. We still need to find a way to get the balloons to Alex the Magician.

  5. LOCATION 3

    “Let’s try looking in.

    [Location 3]

    [Students hold up try manipulative.]

    “Oh, I see some try cards. What is our try?”

    [Looking in Location 3]

    Have co-teacher record on card and place on Velcro board.

    Ask, “Why are we trying this?” [Make “why” gesture] [Because we did not find all 8 balloons last time/Because our goal is to find all 8 of the balloons.] Refer to the Because string to make the causal connections on the Velcro board.

    [Hold up Outcome Fail card]

    [Make “what happened” gesture]

    Is our goal still the same?

    [No. We no longer want to find the balloons because we have found all 8 balloons and they are ruined.]

  6. Explain that we need to call Alex the Magician and let her know what happened.

    Teacher calls magician and says, “Hey Alex. We are really sorry. We went looking for your balloons today. We found them, but they were ruined. You won’t be able to use them for a trick… Okay… That’s fine… Okay. Again, we are so sorry. Okay let us know how it goes. Talk to you later… Bye.”

    “So Alex said she is just going to go out and get new balloons by herself. She said she would let us know how it goes.”

  7. Explain that we can use our Velcro board to help us retell the new part of the story.

    Model retelling the story. Begin with a non-example, retelling the story using by only reading the cards.

    Teaching Modeling: [Speak in a boring voice and add NO detail to the story.] “Goal. Find balloons. First try. Room one. Outcome fail. Next try. Room two. Outcome fail. Last try. Room three. Outcome fail.”

    Explain to students that this is not a good retelling because I just read from the board and did not add any details to make it sound like an actual story, it just sounded like reading from the board.

    Next, model retelling the story appropriately, adding in details and not reading from the board.

    Teacher Modeling: [Use expression and add detail to the story. Include causal connections and finger move.] “When we came in today, we found a note that said our balloons were moved. We couldn’t read where they were moved, WHICH CAUSED (make finger move) us to have the goal of wanting to find the balloons. We decided to look in three different rooms. First we tried ROOM 1. Those balloons were covered in paper mâché, WHICH CAUSED (make finger move) us to TRY to look for the balloons in another room. Next, we tried looking for the balloons in ROOM 2. The balloons in there had been used in a science experiment/lung, WHICH CAUSED (makefinger move) us to TRY to look for the balloons in another room. So finally, we tried ROOM 3. Unfortunately, those balloons were laminated. So, Alex the Magician won’t be able to use these balloons since they have all been ruined.”

    Explain to students throughout how you are making it sound like a story, by using the board only as a guide, instead of reading from it.

5. Picture Book: Part I Apple Trouble! – Small Group

  1. Explain to the students that, “You have been doing a great job of finding goals, tries, outcomes, and causal connections. Now, you’re going to get a chance to practice finding them with a small group in a new story called Apple Trouble!

    Procedure Card

    Prompt students to look at procedure card if they need help.

  2. Before we get started, let’s remind ourselves of what we need to look for, and what we need to do when we are reading a story. You can use your procedure card to help you remember what the steps are as you are working.

    [Hold up procedure card.]

  3. Now that we have reviewed, you should be ready to work with your small group to find goals, tries, outcomes, and causal connections together.

    Explain to students that we will now be switching to “I Help/You Do Together.”

    “We are now going to switch to ‘I Help/You Do Together.’

    [Place these roles onto the Roles Board.]

    Sometimes I will give you a chance to work together. We will call this time: ‘I Help/You Do Together’.”

    [Point to the “I Help/You Do Together” on the Roles Board.]

    “When it is time for ‘I Help/You Do Together,’ you will be talking with each other. You will all work together to figure out the answers. It is important that you talk with one another and tell each other what you are thinking. It is ok not to always agree on the answer, but you will have to talk to figure out the answers together. I will be here only to read and record your work. I will help only if you really are having a hard time talking to each other. You should still be listening with your ears,

    [point to ears]

    watching with your eyes,

    [point to eyes]

    and thinking with your brain.

    [point to brain]

    If you get stuck at any time, try looking at your procedure card for help.”

    [see Gestures for Explaining Roles in Base Appendix, p. 16.]

  4. Say, “Before we get started though, we are going to demonstrate what we mean by “working together with a small group.”

    [Use the Improvised Modeling for Working Together during Paired Practice script on pp. 14-15 of the Base Appendix for this part]

    [Lead- and Co-Teachers will act out a demonstration of what Small Group should look like. Model working together using the anchor chart using a portion of The Ghost with the Halloween Hiccups.]

    • Show the students the “Collaboration Cards” of 2 people trying to carry something heavy and awkward together. Several of the images will be them not working together effectively (one person is doing all the work; no one is doing any work, and not working together) and another will have them succeeding. Explain that they will have to collaborate to do this activity.

    • Lead- and Co-Teacher will improvise how to work together as they read the beginning of Ghost with the Halloween Hiccups. Demonstrate how you talk effectively with one another and how you might politely disagree with one another. After you collaboratively identify the goal and place it on the Velcro Board. Then be sure to record the goal on a yellow thought bubble and place it on the Velcro board.

      [See Improvised Modeling for Working Together during Paired Practice Script on pp. 14-15 of the Base Appendix]

    • Teachers can also demonstrate what not working together effectively looks like.

  5. Break students into small groups and into different rooms.

    Teacher’s Role During Small Group

    This is an opportunity for ontogenetic scaffolding – provide support for the interaction but not the cognition (as much as possible). Also, be sure to offer immediate reinforcement when students work together successfully. Use the “Conversation Starters and Prompts” chart (see Base Appendix) to assist with scaffolding.

    Use the flip chart as needed throughout the activity to give students feedback on how they are doing at working together (working together, one person doing all the work, nobody working, or fighting).

    Prompts for Encouraging Student Collaboration

    If students are not talking to one another (No one is doing the work):

    • Remember that you both have to work and talk to one another to figure out what the goals, tries, and outcomes are.

    • I want you to talk with one another to figure out the goals, tires, and outcomes.

    • Refer students to the Conversation Starters Chart (Base Appendix, p. 12) to help them find things to say to one another.

    If one student is dominating (One person is carrying the load):

    • It looks like one person is carrying the load. Please talk with one another and figure it out together.

    If there is fighting:

    • Remind students that the target is to find the goals, attempts, and outcomes and if students are arguing they won’t be able to reach the target for the lesson.

    • Remind students that they have to work together and cooperate to get the job done.

    • Remind students of behavior rules and pull a star if needed

  6. Begin reading story with plain hedgehog. Ask students to hold the goal, try and outcome cards up when they see the goal, try, or outcome and talk with one another about what they notice. Encourage the use of the manipulatives and thinking aloud. Only stop reading when students hold up marker and have them discuss.

    Prompts for Finding Goals, Tries, Outcomes and Causal Connections

    When students hold up a goal, try, or outcome manipulative, your prompt would be, “Did you see a GOAL/TRY/OUTCOME? Talk about it with your small group and once you agree let’s write it on a card and put it on the board where it belongs.”

    [Have students place cards on board]

    If students are not holding up cards, your prompt should be, “Remember, you should be thinking about whether you hear a goal, try, or outcome. When you hear one you should hold up the appropriate card.”

    If students are not making causal connections, your prompt would be, “Look on your procedure card. Remember, that after we identify a (goal/try) we ask ourselves, “Why?”

    [Make “why” gesture]

    Then we use our Because String to make connections between the events.

    Activity Overview

    Be sure to complete the Velcro board organizer while the story is being read.

    The teacher will place the first item (the apple) on the hedgehogs back. After each try, give one student another item to be placed on the hedgehog’s back taking turns so that each student gets a chance to place an item on the hedgehog.

    1. Nuts

    2. Green pear

    3. Brown leaf

    4. Piece of blue paper

    5. Pink water lily

    6. Blackberries

    The teacher should hold the items for the students, and only hand them to the students to be placed on the hedgehog after the TRY has been identified and charted on the Velcro board.

    Once the goat eats everything off, take the pieces off her back for the end of the story.

    Do not chart the piece of blue paper, pink water lily, or blackberries. Just summarize them and go into the retelling if time permits.

    Once students hold up goal manipulative your prompts would be, “Did you see a GOAL? Talk about it with your group and once you agree let’s write it on an index card and put it on the board where it belongs.”

    [Have students discuss and place cards on board]

    Continue reading.

    As the students hold up try manipulatives, your prompt would be, “Did you see a TRY? Talk about it with your group and once you agree let’s write it on an index card and put it on the board where it belongs.”

    [Have students place cards on board]

    If needed, after students identify a TRY, say “Talk with your group about what you should look for next.” (NOTE: This question is not needed if students identify the outcome without prompting). Students should respond that they will look for the OUTCOME. If they do not, then direct them to look at their procedure card for what the next step in the process is. Once students see that they are now looking for the outcome, continue reading.

    As the students hold up outcome manipulatives, your prompt would be, “Did you see an OUTCOME? Talk about it with your group and once you agree let’s write it on an index card and put it on the board where it belongs.”

    [Have students place cards on board]

    Outcome Flow Chart

    If students are confused about the outcome of the try, use the Outcome Prompt Flowchart in the Base Appendix to guide students.

    Repeat the same procedure for every try and outcome:

    1. Read.

    2. Students hold up a TRY card.

    3. Students discuss what the TRY was with each another,

    4. Complete the laminated try card and have students place on the Velcro board,

    5. Discuss WHY [Make “why” gesture] the hedgehog had to make that try and place the Because Strings on the Velcro board.

    6. Read until students hold up an OUTCOME card.

    7. Students discuss WHAT HAPPENED [Make “what happened” gesture] and discuss what the OUTCOME was with one another.

    8. Complete the laminated outcome card and have students place on Velcro board.

    9. Discuss WHY [Make “why” gesture] the outcome happened with your group and place Because String on the Velcro board.

    10. Continue in this manner throughout the rest of the story.

  7. Remind students of how to use the organizer to retell the story. Have students retell story at the end using the completed organizer if time permits.

  8. Provide evaluative feedback on the students’ performance and behavior.
